Celestial coordinates

Celestial Coordinates are the set of values that, according to a given reference system, give the position of an object on the celestial sphere. There are different celestial Coordinates depending on their origin and plane reference.

Basic concepts

What is latitude?

It is the angle between the parallel of a point and the quator.

Latitude of Medellín

Medellín is located in the geographic center of the Aburrá Valley, on the central mountain range of the Andes, at coordinates 6°13′55″N 75°34′05″W).

What is longitude?

It is the angle between the meridian of a point and the zero meridian (Greenwich). Right ascension and declination coordinates define a coordinate system on the celestial sphere.

Earth meridians

These are the maximum imaginary semicircles of the globe that pass through the north and south poles. By extension, they are also the maximum semicircles that pass through the poles of any sphere or spheroid of reference. They are imaginary lines which main utility is to determine the position (Longitude) of any place on Earth with respect to a reference meridian or meridian 0, adding the Latitude, determined by the parallel that passes through that point.

Equatorial parallel

The Earth's equator, also called the equator line or 0° parallel, is the great circle perpendicular to the axis of rotation of the planet Earth.1 Like any great circle, it defines a plane passing through the center. It divides the planet into northern and southern hemispheres and is located at the same distance from the geographic poles. By definition, the latitude of the Earth's equator is 0°. The equatorial radius is 6378.1 km and the corresponding circumference is 40 075 km.

Types of celestial coordinates

Plane of the ecliptic

The ecliptic coordinate system uses the ecliptic as the fundamental plane. The ecliptic is the path that the Sun appears to follow across the sky over the course of a year. It is also the projection of the Earth's orbital plane on the celestial sphere. The angle of latitude is called the ecliptic latitude, and the longitudinal angle is called the ecliptic longitude. Like the right ascension in the equatorial system, the zero point of ecliptic longitude is the vernal equinox.

The horizontal coordinate system

Uses the observer's local horizon as the fundamental plane. This conveniently divides the sky into an upper hemisphere that can be seen, and a lower hemisphere that remains hidden (behind the Earth itself). The pole of the upper hemisphere is called the zenith, the pole of the lower hemisphere is called the nadir, the angle of an object above or below the horizon is called the elevation, the angle of an object around the horizon (measured from north, eastward) is called azimuth, the horizontal coordinate system is also known as the high azimuthal coordinate system.

Equatorial coordinate system

Is probably the most widely used celestial coordinate system, it is also the one that most closely resembles the geographic coordinate system, since both use the same fundamental plane and the same poles. The projection of the Earth's equator onto the celestial sphere is called the celestial equator. Similarly, the projection of the geographic poles onto the celestial sphere defines the north and south celestial poles.

Difference between the equatorial and geographic coordinate systems

The latter is fixed to the Earth, and rotates with it, the equatorial system is fixed to the stars, so it seems to rotate through the sky along with them; but, of course, it is the Earth that rotates and the sky remains stationary. The celestial equator is an imaginary great circle on the celestial sphere. The celestial equator is the fundamental plane of the equatorial coordinate system, so it is defined as the locus of points with declination zero degrees. It is also the projection of the Earth's equator into the sky.

The celestial equator and the Ecliptic form an angle of 23.5 degrees in the sky, the points where they intersect are known as the vernal and autumnal equinoxes.
